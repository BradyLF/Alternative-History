What Really Happened | August 6 and August 9, 1945

In October 1945, during the final stage of the Second World War, the United States invaded mainland Japan on an unprecedented scale. The attack was the most audacious battle of the Pacific theatre. Resulting in significant casualties for both the Japanese, and the Americans.


The Plan of Attack | Operation Olympic and Operation Coronet

Beginning in October 1945, Operation Olympic was intended to capture the southern third of the southernmost main Japanese island, Kyūshū, with the recently captured island of Okinawa to be used as a staging area. Later, in spring 1946, Operation Coronet was the planned invasion of the Kantō Plain, near Tokyo, on the Japanese island of Honshū. Airbases on Kyūshū captured in Operation Olympic would allow land-based air support for Operation Coronet. If Downfall had taken place, it would have been the largest amphibious operation in human history.

Established Timeline of Events

October 16, 1945

Japan’s geography made an invasion very predictable to Japan. Because of the high tensions between the US and Japan an invasion was imminent and using the strategy of island hopping, as the US did before, was very predictable. The Japanese adjusted their defense strategy to accommodate Operation Olympic and guarded the island of Kyūshū

October 26, 1945

The United States launches a full scale air assault lasting two weeks absolutely pummeling Tokyo.

November 1, 1945

A naval blockade is enacted and airpower is utilized to bring about Japan's capitulation.

November 6, 1945






The US captured air bases in nearby Shanghai, China and Korea which gave the US Army Air Forces a series of forward airbases from which they bombarded Japan into submission.




November 8, 1945

U.S.S.R. declares war on Japan then invades Manchuria.

November 9, 1945






Operation Pastel convinced the Japanese that the Joint Chiefs had rejected the notion of a direct invasion and instead attempted to encircle and bombard Japan. To do this, the US captured bases in Formosa, along the Chinese coast, and in the Yellow Sea area.

November 14, 1945 - May 12, 1946

A United States Amphibious assault is launched on the south of Japan. It is the largest amphibious operation in human history. Allied causalities total 1.12 million deaths and Japanese deaths totaled 1.4 military combatants, including civilians who took up arms.

May 25, 1946

Formal Japanese surrender ceremony on board the MISSOURI in Tokyo Bay as 1,000 carrier-based planes fly overhead; President Truman declares VJ Day.


Analysis of the Invasion

Operation Downfall proved to be an extremely successful attack and ultimately resulted in the surrender of Japan, a reluctant action for the determined Japanese. The casualties on both sides were absurd, but it is speculated that the war would have continued much longer if the US had not invaded with a strong force.
